A **DAR** is the narrative record of your shift: rounds, incidents, visitors, equipment checks, and anything material to the client contract.

### Before you start

- Confirm **which DAR template** your site uses (some tenants use structured sections).
- Gather **times**, **names** (if policy allows), and **incident numbers** if you reference formal incidents.

### Steps

1. From the guard app, open **DAR** or **Reports** and choose **New** (or **Today’s DAR**).
2. Enter **shift identifiers** if prompted (site, post, date).
3. Write **chronological** entries for the reporting period—clear, factual language; avoid speculation.
4. Attach **photos** or **documents** only when allowed by policy and privacy rules.
5. Use **Save draft** periodically if available so you do not lose work.
6. When complete, tap **Submit** (or **Send for review** if your workflow requires supervisor approval first).
7. If the app returns **validation errors**, fix missing required fields and submit again.

### Quality tips

- Reference **post order** requirements you executed (e.g. hourly rounds).
- Link to a separate **incident report** when an event meets incident criteria instead of burying it only in narrative.

### For operators

Supervisor **report center** and **publication** settings control whether DAR content appears in client-facing packages.
