The **general ledger** is where your organization’s financial activity is recorded in accounts, journals, and by time period. Other areas—**invoices**, **bills**, **payroll**—ultimately post here.

### Why it matters

Clean ledger activity supports accurate **financial statements**, **tax filing**, and **management reports**. Most day-to-day work happens in AR, AP, or payroll screens; the ledger is the summary layer accountants care about.

### Tips

Work in the order your controller recommends—often banking and sub-ledgers first, then review the ledger before closing a month.
